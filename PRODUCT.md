# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People managing personal finances who want a practical monthly view of income, spending, savings, investments, budgets, and account movement. They use the product while reviewing transactions, checking financial progress, planning budgets, or adding new financial records.

## Product Purpose

Budgeting Dashboard helps users understand their real financial position from structured transaction data. Success means users can quickly answer: what came in, what went out, what was saved or invested, what changed versus last month, and what needs attention next.

## Brand Personality

Clear, trustworthy, pragmatic. The interface should feel like a focused finance workspace, not a decorative fintech landing page.

## Anti-references

Avoid AI-template signals: purple or amber glow decoration, excessive glassmorphism, gradient text, card grids used as filler, generic SaaS hero copy, inconsistent icon/color vocabularies, and decorative motion that does not explain state.

## Design Principles

1. Make the numbers easy to scan before making the screen visually interesting.
2. Use color for financial meaning, state, and action priority, not decoration.
3. Keep navigation and data management conventional so users can stay in task flow.
4. Favor dense, stable layouts for recurring workflows such as transactions, budgets, and analytics.
5. Let polish come from consistency, hierarchy, and interaction feedback.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Maintain visible keyboard focus, clear labels, sufficient contrast in light and dark themes, reduced-motion behavior, and privacy-first handling for sensitive financial values.
